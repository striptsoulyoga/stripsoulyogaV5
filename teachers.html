Yoga Teacher Tangi Holmes

While attending graduate school in Baltimore, MD, Tangi discovered her love for yoga. It was the way that yoga made her feel physically, mentally, and spiritually, allowing her to have a more balanced and peaceful outlook on life. After graduation, this Mississippi girl moved to L.A. and continued to develop her practice. Wanting to learn and understand yoga more, Tangi enrolled into the Tamal Yoga School and received her Yoga Alliance Certification. During her teacher training program, Tangi found a desire to share her knowledge and made the decision to start teaching.

Tangi believes that yoga is a personal journey for each and every person, and focuses on that as a cornerstone of how she presents herself when teaching. Her classes are designed to be faster-paced, sweaty, fluid, and alignment-focused. She is excited to bring Stript Soul Yoga to the community and to share her passion for yoga with her students.

Yoga Teacher Angela Rose

Angela fell in love with yoga as soon as the soles of her feet touched the mat. Almost 15 plus years after, Angela decided to do something that would change her life forever. Angela completed her 200 hr training at one of her favorite yoga studios, Yoga Salt, with the incredible Tamal Dodge. In addition to teaching yoga, Angela has a BFA in theatre and in the last 5 years began puppeteering. Angela created a children's show called the Mindful Monster where she incorporates yoga to help teach children mindfulness. She believes in the healing power of yoga and mindfulness, they go hand and hand. Angela hopes you leave her class feeling grounded, focused, strong, and with a bit more self-compassion for yourself on and off the mat.

Yoga Teacher Allyson Lynch

Allyson Lynch, is a small-town girl born and raised in Sacramento, California. After graduating from Sacramento State University with a Bachelor’s in Recreation, Parks and Tourism Administration she found herself moving to Los Angeles where she began her dream job working at L.A’s premiere Stand Up Paddleboard Yoga studio “YOGAqua” on the beautiful ocean in Marina Del Rey. Getting to be a part of facilitating memorable experiences where one has the opportunity to be able to connect with nature and challenge their physical strength is what she lives for!

Being a fitness enthusiast she enjoyed running, competing in marathons, triathlons and Tough Mudder competitions until a knee injury forced her to slow down and find a new workout routine. Discovering Yoga became her saving grace and an outlet for stress and her restless energy. Allyson has completed her 200-hour RYT under world renowned Yoga Teacher and Yoga Salt owner Tamal Dodge, deepening her understanding and appreciation for the practice of Yoga. Allyson is also a certified SUP Yoga Instructor, Level-1 World Paddle Association Instructor and Schwinn Certified Spin instructor.

Allyson's Yoga classes can be seen as all-level Vinyasa classes,connecting breath to movement. It's a flowing practice where you move to the rhythm of your own breath and some good music. Her classes are designed to help you relax and restore your body while still building strength and flexibility. Being an active individual she understands the important balance between a powerful, fast-paced Vinyasa class and also slowing things down for a gentle Yin-style flow where it’s all about softness, stillness and being kind to our bodies. Taking class with her you are sure to experience both!

Yoga Teacher Jenni Kim

Jenny Kim grew up in Denver, CO and stepped into her first yoga class when she was 17. It was the first thing she'd ever pursued as a young adult by herself, for herself, for no other reason than feeling drawn to it. She immediately fell in love the the practice - the challenge, the growth, the connection between soul and body. She cultivated a consistent yoga practice, moved to Los Angeles, and worked in yoga studios and wellness programs while attending undergraduate and graduate school at USC. She graduated from Tamal Dodge's 200 hour yoga teacher training in the Spring of 2018. Jenny Kim's classes are challenging, playful, and dynamic. During the week, Jenny enjoys working as an Occupational Therapist specializing in Hand Therapy. The yoga mat sets her free.

Yoga Teacher Marion Shively

Growing up in San Francisco, Marian is a California native. She has played competitive sports her whole life, but never found a physical activity she truly fell in love with. While living in the Bay Area, she was always hesitant to try yoga as she did not consider herself flexible and felt she did not have the "right" body for the practice. However, after she moved to Los Angeles for graduate school at LMU, she discovered yoga and fell in love immediately. She quickly learned yoga was for all people, no matter what their body type or level of flexibility. She completed her 200 hour teacher training with Tamal Dodge in the spring of 2018 and has been teaching ever since. For Marian, teaching yoga has been an honor and a gift. She hopes to help others, both physically and mentally, through her vinyasa style yoga classes. She encourages anyone and everyone to try her yoga classes, no matter what their experience with yoga is!

Yoga Teacher Emily Fuller

When you have an extremely long, nerve racking, or stressful day ahead, Emily is exactly who you want to wake up and start your day with. Born and raised in West Hollywood, Emily has deep routes in the community and is thrilled to be back where it all started. Currently, Emily is an LA County lifeguard, enjoys dog walking and all aquatic activities. As a passionate vinyasa yoga teacher, she wishes to bring her positivity and excitement into your weekly routine. Emily believes yoga is a practice that can benefit anyone and everyone regardless of labels like age, athleticism, flexibility, and disabilities. Through the physical practice of traditional vinyasa and mindful movements, she continues to explore and expand upon her knowledge of how yoga can develop and transform individuals on and off the mat. Emily's classes will leave you feeling light hearted, clear minded and powerfully prepared for whatever is next on the agenda. Ultimately, Emily wishes to empower and elevate others the way so many people in the yoga community have done for her.
